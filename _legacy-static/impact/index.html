Impact

Growth should leave something good behind.

Boho uses powerful digital tools, including AI, while staying honest about their footprint. That means efficient workflows, lean websites, transparent impact reporting, and a portion of profits directed toward verified climate action and pollinator-friendly causes.

Climate-aware workflows Lean website practices Pollinator support Annual impact note

We do not treat impact language like a mood board. If we say something here, it should be measurable, observable, or honestly limited.

Climate & Tools Acknowledgment

Digital work has a footprint.

Websites, hosting, APIs, analytics, AI-assisted research, storage, and media all consume resources. We use these tools because they are useful, not because they are weightless.

What we measure

  • Page weight and asset bloat
  • Dependency sprawl and unnecessary scripts
  • Hosting and deployment choices where they are within our control
  • Hours and dollars allocated to verified climate or pollinator support

What we reduce

  • Heavy front-end stacks when a lighter static approach will do
  • Redundant plugins, trackers, and third-party scripts
  • Unnecessary image weight and autoplay clutter
  • Operational fog that leads to wasteful rebuild cycles later

What we fund

A portion of profits is intended for verified climate action and pollinator-friendly causes. That support is documented through the annual impact note rather than inflated into fake certification theater.

Pollinator resources

The bee is not just branding.

Pollinator support matters because it links the brand system back to something tangible. The goal is to back projects that are grounded, local when possible, and actually beneficial to pollinator habitats.

Annual Impact Note

Short, specific, and honest.

Each annual note should show what was measured, what changed, and what was funded. If there are gaps, they should be stated plainly.
