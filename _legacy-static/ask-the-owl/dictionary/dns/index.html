DNS

DNS

DNS is the system that points your domain name to the right website, email, and other online services.

Plain-English version

DNS is the routing layer that tells the internet where your domain should go.

Why it matters

Provider transfers, site launches, and email setups can break if DNS records are missing, wrong, or locked inside someone else's account.

What to ask your current provider

  • Where are the DNS records managed?
  • Who has permission to edit them?
  • What changes will a migration require?
