Provider lock-in

Provider lock-in

Provider lock-in happens when a vendor controls key accounts, files, tools, or access in a way that makes it difficult for the client to leave.

Shortcut Fox Warning

Lock-in is not the same as competent stewardship. If leaving is artificially hard, that is a business risk.

Why it matters

When a provider controls the registrar, DNS, repository, CMS, analytics, and billing under their own accounts, the client may not truly own the website layer.

What to ask your current provider

  • Which accounts belong directly to the client?
  • Where are the files and repositories?
  • Can access be transferred without rebuilding the whole stack?
