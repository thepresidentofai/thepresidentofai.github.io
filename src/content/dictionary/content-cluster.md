---
title: Content cluster
category: content strategy
shortDefinition: A content cluster is a group of related pages that support a core topic or service from different angles.
plainEnglish: It turns scattered articles into a more coherent ecosystem.
whyItMatters: Clusters help content support service pages and authority-building goals instead of competing with itself.
example: A provider-transfer service page might be supported by glossary entries, migration checklists, and plain-English guides that all connect back to the core offer.
relatedTerms:

relatedServices:

---
Clusters are useful because they create context and connective tissue. They help one strong page do more work by surrounding it with purposeful support.

The key is restraint. A good cluster feels intentional and navigable, not like a hundred lightly-related pages published because someone needed volume.
