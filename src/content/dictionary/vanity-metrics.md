shortDefinition: Vanity metrics are numbers that look impressive without helping a team make better decisions.

whyItMatters: Reporting can feel active while still leaving the team no wiser about what should happen next.
example: A dashboard celebrating raw traffic spikes without explaining lead quality, page context, or next actions is often leaning on vanity metrics.

Vanity metrics are attractive because they are easy to screenshot and easy to celebrate.

Good reporting stays close to decisions. It helps a team choose what to fix, what to expand, what to stop doing, and what result actually mattered.
